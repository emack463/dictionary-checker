Article 3 -- No TitleThe Scotsman (1817-1858)At the last Quarter Sessions for the County of Cornwall several persons stating themselves to be seamen travelling in search of employment at Falmouth and who were reduced to the necessity of begging were discharged after being admonished not to be found again so offending in the county or they would be more dealt with Such as had set to be transmitted to their respective parishes One poor fellow an American who stated that lie liad served many years in the navy on being cautioned against being brought again before the Court as vagrant replied Bless your Worships am an old man and have spent my life at sea-I don know any other way of getting my bread can get ship so must starve was only travelling in search of vessel to take me out of England
